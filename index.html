    What We Do

    AMDA-Nepal provides various healthcare services and programs to improve the quality of life for underprivileged communities.

    Hospital/Clinic Activities

    Providing quality healthcare services through our network of hospitals and clinics across Nepal.

    Academic Programs

    Conducting training, workshops, and educational programs for healthcare professionals.

    Partnership and Collaboration

    Working with local and international organizations to expand our reach and impact.

    Citizen Charter

    Promoting healthcare rights and responsibilities among citizens for better health outcomes.

    Emergency Relief

    Providing immediate medical assistance during natural disasters and emergencies.

    Social Responsibility

    Implementing community-based programs to address social determinants of health.

    AMDA Nepal medical team providing care at a rural health camp

    Dedicated to Healthcare Excellence since 1989

    Association of Medical Doctors of Asia-Nepal (AMDA-Nepal) was established in 1989 as a chapter of AMDA-International. Legally registered in the government office as an NGO in March 4, 1991, it is a humanitarian, non-profit making, non-political organization committed to providing quality healthcare services to underserved communities.
